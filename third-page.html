National Asian American Survey (NAAS) Post-Election Survey, 2016

  • The NAAS 2016 Post-Election Survey was fielded from November 2016 through February 2017.
    - The project is funded by National Science Foundation, Ford Foundation, California Immigrant Research Initiative, Office of the President, University of California, Wallace H. Coulter Foundation
  • N= 4362 (Asians and Asian Americans)

Dependent Variable: Impression of the BLM Movement

  • “Do you have a very favorable, somewhat favorable, somewhat unfavorable, or very unfavorable impression of [NAME]?”
  • [The Black Lives Matter Movement, which seeks racial equality, and campaigns against violence and systemic racism toward black people]
  • 0 – some unfavorable, very unfavorable, no opinion, never heard
  • 1 – very favorable, some favorable

Independent Variables

  • Social contact
  • “In your daily life, how much contact do you, personally, have with people who are…” (Black or African American)
  • 1 – no contact at all
  • 2 – only a little contact
  • 3 – some contact
  • 4 – a lot of contact

Control Variables

  • Demographics
    – Gender (Female = 1 )
    – Age (18 - 24 = 1; 25 - 34 = 2; 35 - 49 = 3; 50 - 64 = 4; 65 or over = 5)
    – State (blue state = 1)
    – Generational status (U.S. born = 1)
  • SES
    – Education (no high school degree = 1; high school degree = 2; college degree or bachelor’s degree = 3; graduate or professional degree = 4)
